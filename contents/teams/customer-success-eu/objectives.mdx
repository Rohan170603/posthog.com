### Skill discoverability <TeamMember name="Dana Zou" photo />
We're all building skills, but it feels like whether you know about a skill all depends on whether you saw it on Slack. The result is ~10 versions of the same workflow, and we're spending time rebuilding very similar skills. I'll work on this from three angles:
 - 1-3 canonical skills for simple-but-manual tasks where a shared version works. e.g. billing page visit analysis, chasing unpaid invoices, renewal prep, and a first-pass account review.
 - A `find-skills` skill: Claude checks the store for an existing team skill before building a workflow from scratch, or mid-task ("is there already a skill for what I'm doing?").
 - The store as the default home: every skill shared in Slack lands in the store, and I'll regularly re-share and remind people of what's in there!

**We'll know we're successful when**: canonical skills are in the store and being used by the team, find-skills is shipped and in everyone's setup, and all skills shared in Slack are in our Skills store
