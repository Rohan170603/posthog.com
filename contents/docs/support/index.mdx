Support gives you one inbox for every customer conversation, whether it arrives from your app, your email, or a GitHub issue. Because PostHog already knows what's happening in your product, each ticket comes with the sender's [session replay](/docs/session-replay), events, and errors attached, so you can see what went wrong instead of trying to reproduce it. Those same conversations feed [Self-driving](/docs/self-driving), which groups recurring issues into pull requests you review and merge.

## Where you can use it

Support does different things depending on where you work. You read and reply to customers in the [Support inbox](/docs/support/inbox). The other surfaces are for reviewing the Self-driving work your tickets generate.

## How Support works with Self-driving

Your support conversations are a [signal source](/docs/self-driving/inbox/sources) for [Self-driving](/docs/self-driving): recurring issues become reports, and when there's a code fix, a pull request you review and merge.

You read and reply to customers in the [Support inbox](/docs/support/inbox), and review and merge the Self-driving work in the [Self-driving inbox](/docs/self-driving/inbox). See the [Self-driving docs](/docs/self-driving) for the full picture.
