<QuestLogItem title="Enable Support and add the widget" subtitle="Where tickets begin" icon="IconToggle">

The first step is to enable Support and add the in-app widget to your site, so customer conversations start arriving as tickets. Every widget ticket automatically attaches the sender's [session replay](/session-replay), [events](/docs/product-analytics/capture-events), and [identity](/docs/product-analytics/identify), so you always have the full story.

Building your own interface instead? The [JavaScript API](/docs/support/javascript-api) gives you the same backend without the default widget.

<OSButton variant="primary" asLink to="/docs/support/widget">Set up the widget</OSButton>

</QuestLogItem>

<QuestLogItem title="Connect your other channels" subtitle="Email, Slack, and GitHub" icon="IconMessage">

Meet customers wherever they are. Beyond the widget, conversations can arrive from [email](/docs/support/email), [Slack](/docs/support/slack), and [GitHub](/docs/support/github), and every channel syncs both ways so you answer from a single inbox.

<QuestLogItem title="Work your tickets" subtitle="Your team's inbox" icon="IconNotebook">

The [Support inbox](/docs/support/inbox) is where your team lives. Open any ticket and the customer's replay, recent events, exceptions, and past conversations are right there, so you resolve issues without the back-and-forth. Assign, tag, snooze, and track SLAs from one view.

<QuestLogItem title="Automate assignment, tags, and SLAs" subtitle="Rules you control" icon="IconDecisionTree">

Let rules handle the repetitive parts. [Workflows](/docs/support/workflows) are rules you control – no autonomous AI involved. For example:

- Set SLAs based on channel or priority
- Auto-assign new tickets to a role
- Tag tickets by customer email domain
- Reopen a ticket when the customer replies

<OSButton variant="primary" asLink to="/docs/support/workflows">Set up workflows</OSButton>

</QuestLogItem>

<QuestLogItem title="Turn tickets into fixes" subtitle="Support feeds Self-driving" icon="IconMagic">

Support isn't just an inbox – it's a signal source. Recurring issues in your conversations become [Self-driving](/docs/self-driving) reports, and when there's a code fix, a pull request you review and merge. Answering customers still happens in the Support inbox; you review these fixes in the Self-driving inbox.

<QuestLogItem title="Migrating from another tool?" subtitle="Bring your tickets with you" icon="IconUpload">

Bring your history with you so the inbox starts with context instead of an empty slate. The [Zendesk](/docs/support/imports/zendesk) import (beta) brings over:

- Full message threads, including internal notes
- Customer names and emails
- Tags, statuses, and priorities
- Attachments and original timestamps
